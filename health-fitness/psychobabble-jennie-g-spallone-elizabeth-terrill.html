Dr. Katherine Cook, Ph. D, struggles with frequent black outs, memory loss, and impulsive behavior. Items she's not purchased turn up in her desk drawer and refrigerator. After neurological and physical tests come back negative, the Chicago domestic violence psychologist fears she is losing her mind.

As Dr. Kate wrestles with her health situation, a serial killer of pedophiles is on the loose. The FBI is poised to pounce on the Case, while CPD detectives Maggie O'Connor and Monroe Jackson plod through a myriad of false leads. Is it the strip club bouncer, a grandmother with a grudge, or the psychologist's ex-husband? 

Then Dr. Robert Lassiter reveals to Kate that at least one member of the Loved Ones of Pedophiles support group they cohost may be involved in the killings. Now the psychologist must decide whether to betray the anonymity of their members, or allow a serial killer to walk free. In either case, she will be forced to confront childhood truths she's kept hidden for two decades -- even from herself.
