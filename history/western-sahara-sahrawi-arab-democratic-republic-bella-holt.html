In the late 19th century, this vast territory was inhabited mainly by nomadic tribes, known as the 'Saharawi people', and subject to Spanish colonial rule. Following its designation as a 'Non-Self-Governing Territory' under the Charter of the United Nations and increasing international pressure to decolonize the territory, Spain unilaterally withdrew from its role as administering power in 1975, and signed a secret deal to hand the territory over to a joint Morocco-Mauritania administration.
